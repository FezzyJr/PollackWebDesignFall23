Childhood and Education

Roald Dahl was born in 1916 at Villa Marie, Fairwater Road, in Llandaff, Cardiff, Wales. Norwegians Harald Dahl was his father and Sofie Magdalene Dahl was his mother. Roald Dahl attended The Cathedral School, Llandaff. At age 8, he and 4 of his friends got in trouble with the headmaster after putting a dead mouse in a jar of gobstoppers at the local sweet shop, which was owned by an old woman named Mrs Pratchett. Dahl transferred to St Peter's boarding school in Weston-super-Mare. From 1929, when he was 13, Dahl attended Repton School in Derbyshire.

Afterschool in the Army

In November 1939, Dahl joined the Royal Air Force as an aircraftman. he was accepted for flight training with sixteen other men, and only 3 survived the war. After six months of training on Hawker Harts, he was choosen as a pilot officer on August 24, 1940. He was said ready to join a squadron and fight the enemy. Dahl was ordered to fly his Gladiator, but he could not find the airstrip. He was running low on fuel it was almost night , he was forced to a land in the desert. The aircraft crashed. Dahl's skull was fractured and his nose was smashed and he was got blinded for a short time. He was able to drag himself away from the crash and passed out.

Writing life

His first children's book was The Gremlins, released in 1943, about goofy tiny creatures that were part of Royal Air Force.
