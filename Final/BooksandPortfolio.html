Roald Dahl top 5 Books

Website by: Carlos Pollack

5. George’s Marvellous Medicine

The book is about a 8 year old George Kranky who lives on a farm with his mom, dad and his maternal grandma. George's grandmother bosses him around and bullies him. He is supposed to give her medicine at eleven o'clock. So he decided to make a magic medicine to replace his grandma's normal medicine. And things don't turn out the way he expected... The book has sadly not won any awards but it is a very popular and funnty book for kids to read.

4. James and the Giant Peach

James and the Giant Peach is about a young English orphan boy who enters a gigantic, magical peach, and has a wild and crazy around-the-world adventure with seven magicn garden bugs he meets. They set off on a journey to escape from James's two mean and cruel aunts. The book has won the Massachusetts Children’s Award in 1982.

3. The BFG

The BFG won 4 awards and it is his best selling book of all time. The awards were: 1982 Federation of Children’s Book Groups Award,1984 Deutsche Jugendliteraturpreis, 1986 International Board on Books for Young People awards for Norwegian and German translations, and 1997 Wins Good Book Guide “Best Books of the Past 20 Years." It is about a friendly giant known as The BFG who collects and gives good dreams to children, and an orphan named Sophie. Together, The BFG, Sophie, and the Queen of England, help to get rid the world of the child-eating giants.

2. Matilda

Matilda won the 1988 Children's Book Award by kids from all around the world. A girl gifted with a great intellect and psychic powers uses both to deal with her cruel, distant family and free her kind teacher from their sadistic headmistress.

1. Charlie and the Chocolate Factory

Charlie and The Chocolate Factory won many awards. Here are some of the awards he won: 1972 New England Round Table of Children’s Librarians Award, 1973 Surrey School award, and in 2000 he wins Millennium Children’s Book Award and Blue Peter Book Award. It is about a young boy named Charlie Bucket, who wins a golden ticket and gets the opportunity to tour the mysterious and magical chocolate factory owned by the eccentric Willy Wonka. He eventally wins the factory after resisting all the temptations.
